**Role:** Web Design, Web Development, UI, UX, Hosting

Designed and developed a new website for **"ddd – dramaturgie danse dialogue"**, a project initiated by the **Association Vaudoise de Danse Contemporaine (AVDC)**. The platform was built using **WordPress** with the **Gutenberg block editor**, allowing the AVDC team to easily update and adapt the site over time.

From the start, I was involved in shaping the platform’s **content structure** and **user experience**, working closely with AVDC to translate the values and complexity of the _ddd_ initiative into an accessible digital format. The goal was to reflect the open, collaborative, and experimental spirit of the project through a clear, minimalistic, responsive, and elegant design.

The site presents the **ddd rencontres**—a series of gatherings and workshops exploring dramaturgy in dance and performance—not as a strict discipline, but as a fluid, intuitive practice that moves between people, ideas, and creative processes. These events are open to a broad spectrum of practitioners in the performing and visual arts, from choreographers and performers to lighting designers, scenographers, and cultural workers.

The platform includes sections for **current news**, **upcoming events**, **archives**, and **resources**, all structured to evolve alongside the project. It's hosted at **Infomaniak**, a Swiss-based provider committed to ecological and ethical hosting, ensuring data sovereignty and reliability.

This site is part of a broader collaboration between AVDC and numerous Swiss cultural institutions and festivals, supported by public and private foundations.
